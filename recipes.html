Nordic Taste

Scandinavian Carpaccio

    ingredients
  • 600g of salmon;
  • 0.2 g of saffron;
  • 2 tbsp safflower;
  • 6 pcs. Star anise;
  • 150g lobster concentrate (broth);
  • 1 vegetable stock cube (instead of salt);
  • 1 cup of white wine;
  • 1 large orange;
  • Vegetables for broth: tomato, onion, chili, fennel

The first step will be to thoroughly wash and cut the vegetables from which we will cook the broth, but here we have a free hand to choose our ingredients, because it depends only on the availability and our taste which of them we choose. Put the chopped vegetables in a pot and fry it (photo3), pour in white wine. Then add anise, lobster concentrate, safflower, broth cube and pour 2 liters of water. Cook over medium heat until the vegetables are completely overcooked (keep the level of about 2 liters all the time). In the meantime, we cut our salmon into cubes and put it in the oven, we keep it at 55 degrees for about 40 minutes. I emphasize that you should try to get fresh fish, if not, buy frozen, in these two cases do not worry, but if you are not sure about freshness, use higher cooking temperatures (at the expense of quality of course). When our soup is ready, strain it through a fine strainer. Put the strained soup on low heat once more and add the saffron and grated orange peel and bring it to the boil again. We put the salmon on deep plates and pour the soup over it.
